Virtual reality and rehabilitation

Through testing it has been shown that the rehabilitation process is much faster when using Virtual Reality as a tool. Two groups were tested, one using Virtual Reality as a rehabilitation tool and the other group used traditional rehabilitation methods.

After two months the results showed that the Virtual Reality group had improved their motor functions more than the other group.

When comparing the average total time each group used, the Virtual Reality group used 24 minutes less than the traditional group.

Why VR?

What is VR?

VR stands for Virtual Reality. Virtual reality allows you to experience something unique. Through a combination of hardware, software and sensory synchronicity, VR brings you closer to another reality. You get a feeling of truly being present in the given environment.

The VR software and hardware market size is expected to grow continuously. It is expected that global revenue from VR hardware and software will generate over $20 billion by 2020. This is a huge jump over just 4 years

The general population is becoming more interested in VR technology, in 2017 alone over 12 million VR headsets were sold, it's expected to increase to 68 million by 2020.

This graph shows how interested people are in virtual reality on a scale of 1-5, where 1 is not at all interested and 5 is very interested. Nearly 30% has expressed that they are very interested. The general population is becoming more interested.

The use of VR is becoming more widespread, not only for gaming, but its opportunites are endless.
